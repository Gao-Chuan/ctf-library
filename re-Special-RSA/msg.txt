Although two nodes are physically connected by a transmission medium such as cable or air, we need to remember that the data-link layer controls how the medium is used. However, in a internetwork such as the Internet we cannot make a datagram reach its destination using only IP addresses. As we will see in Chapter 13, the broadcast linklayer addresses in the most common LAN, Ethernet, are 48 bits, all 1s, that are presented as 12 hexadecimal digits separated by colons.
